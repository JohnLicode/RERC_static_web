Categories of Research Ethics Review

Depending on the potential risk level and nature of the study, research projects undergo different types of ethical evaluation. Below are the three main categories:

Exempt Review

For studies with negligible or no risk to participants, usually involving non-sensitive information or data already in the public domain.

Expedited Review

For projects involving minimal risks, such as secondary data analysis or minor modifications to previously approved research.

Full Board Review

Required for research that poses more than minimal risk, involves vulnerable groups, or includes sensitive/intervention-based procedures.

How to Use the System

Follow these steps to submit your research protocol and track its progress through the review process.

Sign Up

Create your account by filling out the registration form with your basic details to access the system.

Submit your Protocol

Upload the required documents and complete the checklist to submit your research for review.

Track your Review Status

Follow your submission’s progress in real time and get notified of any updates or requirements.

Decision and Feedback

View the outcome of the evaluation and access any remarks or official notices provided through your dashboard.
