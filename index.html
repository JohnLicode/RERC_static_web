Ensuring Ethical Standards in Research

Categories of Research Ethics Review

Depending on the potential risk level and nature of the study, research projects undergo different types of ethical evaluation. Below are the three main categories:

Exempt Review

For studies with negligible or no risk to participants, usually involving non-sensitive information or data already in the public domain.

Expedited Review

For projects involving minimal risks, such as secondary data analysis or minor modifications to previously approved research.

Full Board Review

Required for research that poses more than minimal risk, involves vulnerable groups, or includes sensitive/intervention-based procedures.
